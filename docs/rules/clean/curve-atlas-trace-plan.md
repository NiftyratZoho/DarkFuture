## Purpose

Faithful curve layout is the current tactical-engine blocker.

The rulebook prose explains how to treat curves, but the exact forward links, uneven lane alignment, contact zones, and hazard slots must be traced from diagrams or component scans. This plan defines the work needed to turn curve diagrams into canonical `trackSpace` data.

## Source Review

Page 10 is sufficient for the even/uneven lane rule and outside-divider method, but it is an example rather than a complete board trace.

Pages 74-76 confirm the component families used by generation: straight, broad 60-degree bend, and tight 90-degree corner, each with left/right direction. These pages are usable for generation metadata and UI thumbnails.

Pages 78-79 confirm road-hazard placement constraints: straight sections have three spaces, curve sides expose two to five ordered edge slots, and railroad crossings use central straight spaces or curve end spaces.

The current scans are not yet sufficient to safely enumerate every curve `trackSpace.forward` and `driftOutward` link without a manual overlay/trace. A faithful implementation should therefore treat curve graph data as blocked until the atlas is traced from page images or better photos/scans of the physical track pieces.

## Required Atlas Pieces

Minimum tactical atlas:

- `straight`
- `broadCurveLeft`
- `broadCurveRight`
- `tightCornerLeft`
- `tightCornerRight`

Right-hand curves should be generated by mirroring left-hand traces only after the left-hand data is verified against examples.

## Track Space Fields

Every curve `trackSpace` must define:

- `id`
- `pieceId`
- `lanePair`
- `curveSpaceKind`: `even` or `uneven`
- `outsideDividerIndex`
- `forward`
- `driftOutward`
- `driftInward`: usually illegal while final position remains on curve
- `contactAnchor`
- `contactZone`
- `frontOrdinal`
- `travelDirection`
- `render`
- `source`

`render` is only for simple box/wedge display. It must not drive movement legality.

## Trace Method

1. Choose one printed left-hand curve diagram as the authoritative base for each curve type.
2. Number lane pairs from inside to outside and record the mapping to project lane-pair ids.
3. Mark every visible space divider on the outside edge.
4. Assign each car-sized legal position a `trackSpace` id.
5. For each position, trace the normal forward destination using the outside-divider rule.
6. For each position, trace outward drift destination after the forward move.
7. Mark inward drift as illegal unless the forward move leaves the curve onto a straight.
8. Add `frontOrdinal` values so staggered curve positions can be compared for sideswipe legality.
9. Add contact anchors and explicit contact-zone sets for even and uneven lane positions.
10. Mirror verified left-hand data to right-hand data.
11. Recheck mirrored data against right-hand diagrams or track-generation examples.

## Validation Examples

Each example should become a test case.

### Movement

- A vehicle moving straight through a curve follows its logical lane pair.
- A vehicle in an uneven pair uses the outside divider to determine its next position.
- A drift on a curve moves outward only.
- A drift from even to uneven aligns to the outside divider.
- A vehicle leaving a curve onto a straight may drift in either direction if otherwise legal.

### U-turns

- U-turns are illegal fully within a curve.
- U-turns may be legal across a curve/straight edge.
- A U-turn contact zone spans six lanes.

### Contact Zones

- Curve contact zones use curve grid anchors, not rendered car overlap.
- Uneven curve contact zones use outside lane dividers.
- Sideswipe legality compares front positions, not sprite angles.
- Head-on and shunt classification on curves uses lane direction and lane overlap.

### Track Generation

- Initial generation starts with three straights and continues until ten sections.
- Curve generation chooses broad/tight and left/right as defined by dice rolls.
- A curve is followed by an automatic straight.
- Continuous generation maintains the seven-section active track window.
- Two same-direction curves force the next curve opposite where applicable.

## Definition of Done

Curve atlas tracing is done when:

- Left and right broad curves have explicit `trackSpace` graphs.
- Left and right tight corners have explicit `trackSpace` graphs.
- Forward, outward drift, boundary U-turn, contact-zone, and front-order data are present.
- Track-generation data can instantiate every required piece.
- Tests cover each rulebook movement/contact example.
- The UI can render the atlas with simple boxes/wedges and show legal-move/debug overlays from the data.
